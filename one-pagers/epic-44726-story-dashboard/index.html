The epic was restructured in early June: automated testing was promoted to its own feature, a Future Scope feature now holds deferred work, and a Route to Production feature gathers the deployment work — its original pipeline story (45960) has moved to In QA, and it has since grown into a full Azure deployment breakdown covering Key Vault and secrets, a DbUp migration runner, Private Link SQL connectivity, Azure Files image storage, App Service hardening, observability, auth, and CI/CD. Under the field-app feature a further wave of stories was raised: production-parity stubs now mirror the RP sign-off, mapping, offline mapping, workforce, and in-app audit work alongside the earlier login and work-order stubs, and new scope stories cover feature flags to hide non-MVP surfaces, photo limits and image size, a photo gallery, job-versus-man hours, and sign-off questions. The backlog now stands at 64 stories and spikes across the eight features, with nine built or planned stories Blocked — view assigned work, the test-environment set-up, the in-app audit story, the Woodplan RP-sheet logic, the soft-delete spike, the non-MVP feature flags, photo limits, and the two on-prem API stories. The first stories have reached Done, Awaiting QA, In QA, and Ready to Deploy.

Coverage tells a leaner story than at the last review. The ViewModel unit suites and the Maestro UI-flow suite that earlier stood behind the field-app stories have been removed from the repo; in their place the current branch adds 21 JVM logic tests covering completion-question rules and the offline map and PMTiles stack. These are authored but were not run this cycle — they sit on the edit-pole-map-improvements branch ahead of main and await their first CI run, so none are yet marked verified green.
